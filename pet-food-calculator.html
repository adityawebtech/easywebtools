How Our Pet Food Calculator Works

Pet nutrition can be confusing. Our web tool was created for US pet families who want practical, accurate calculations based on veterinary best practices—not sales hype or guesswork. The calculator uses Resting Energy Requirement (RER) and Maintenance Energy Requirement (MER) equations like those used in American veterinary practice, tailored by species, age, activity, and weight.

When you enter your pet's species, exact or approximate age, weight, and lifestyle, our calculator references published nutrition guidelines (from AAFCO, AVMA, and ASPCA), instantly calculating calories and converting to typical food amounts per day for each pet type.

Science Behind the Tool

This calculator is based on:

  • Resting Energy Requirement (RER): RER = 70 × (body weight in kg)0.75
  • Maintenance Energy Requirement (MER): Multiplies RER by a species- and life-stage-dependent factor (1.2–3.0+)
  • Species-specific conversion tables for birds, rabbits, guinea pigs, fish, etc.
  • Portion recommendations rely on typical US-brand pet food densities (foods per cup, per can, or per typical gram/pellet serving)

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I’m feeding my pet enough?

Track your pet’s weight monthly and ensure ribs are easily felt but not visible. Our calculator gives a trusted starting range, but always consult your veterinarian for fine-tuning. Regular weigh-ins are proven to prevent silent weight gain or loss.

What’s the healthiest diet for my pet?

Choose foods formulated to meet AAFCO standards for your pet’s species and age. Mix up fresh, commercially formulated, or prescription diets as advised by your vet.

Can I use this calculator for wet/raw diets?

You can—it provides results in grams as well as cups. For home-cooked or raw, use the kcal total, weighing accordingly. Always check with a veterinary nutritionist first when changing diet types.

How often should I feed my pet?

Most dogs and cats do best with two meals per day. Puppies, kittens, and small mammals may require three or four, while some reptiles or fish may only need feeding every 1–3 days. The tool helps guide by species.

Is this tool safe for pets with health problems?

Only as a general reference. Pets with diabetes, kidney disease, allergies, or on special diets must follow your veterinarian's personalized advice above all else.

Should food be changed as my pet ages?

Yes! Caloric needs drop with most senior pets. Our calculator flags when a change of group could impact portion sizes.

How accurate is the data?

Our calculators implement published US nutrition protocols, and our team reviews/updates all numbers yearly in July with the latest data from AAFCO, AVMA, and ASPCA.
