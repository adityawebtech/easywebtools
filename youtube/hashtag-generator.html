What Are YouTube Hashtags?

YouTube hashtags are clickable keywords that help categorize your videos. You’ve probably seen them above a video title or in the description — things like #tech, #shorts, or #DIY. When viewers click them, they’re taken to a feed of related content using the same tag. Neat, right?


Why Do YouTube Hashtags Matter?

Think of hashtags like bridges — they connect your video to a wider topic or trend. If you post a video about productivity and use #productivity, users exploring that hashtag can find your content more easily. It’s especially helpful for smaller or newer creators trying to grow their reach.


For example, let’s say you post a YouTube Short about time-saving Chrome extensions. If you use hashtags like #productivity, #ChromeHacks, or #techshorts, you instantly boost the video’s visibility to folks interested in those topics.

When and Where to Use Hashtags

You can use hashtags in three spots: the video title, the description, or both. YouTube recommends not stuffing too many. Keep it clean — usually 3–5 is enough. Also, avoid banned or misleading hashtags (like #giveaway if there’s no giveaway).


How Is It Different from Tags?

Good question! Tags (meta keywords in upload settings) help with YouTube’s backend algorithm understanding. Hashtags, on the other hand, are visible to viewers and create public topic pages. Both are useful — but hashtags are more interactive.
