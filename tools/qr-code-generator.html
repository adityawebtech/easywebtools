What is a QR Code?

QR code stands for “Quick Response code.” It’s a type of matrix barcode (or two-dimensional barcode) originally designed for the automotive industry in Japan. However, its ability to store a large amount of data and be quickly scanned using a smartphone camera or QR scanner made it universally adopted in countless sectors.

From menus and business cards to product packaging, advertisements, and payment systems, QR codes are everywhere—and for good reason. They help users access websites, download apps, connect to Wi-Fi, share contact details, and so much more, all with a quick scan.
